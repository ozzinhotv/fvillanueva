Carlos Villanueva (1900–1975) revolutionized Venezuelan architecture through his integration of modern principles with tropical sensibilities. His masterwork, Ciudad Universitaria de Caracas, stands as a UNESCO World Heritage site and testament to his innovative vision.

Beyond individual buildings, Villanueva conceived architecture as a synthesis of arts, collaborating with renowned artists like Alexander Calder and Carlos Cruz-Diez to create spaces that transcended traditional boundaries between disciplines.

His residence, now a museum, preserves not only his personal sanctuary but also his design philosophy—a harmonious blend of function, form, and artistic expression that continues to inspire architects worldwide.
